Grammar Focus 1

Past of "To Be"

To describe people, places, and feelings in the past, we use Was and Were.

WAS I / He / She / It
WERE You / We / They
  • "He was a baby."
  • "I was in Smallville."
  • "They were happy in the city."

Interactive Challenge

Heat Vision

Use your Heat Vision! Click the WRONG verb to fix the sentence.

"His parents was from a farm. They were very good people."

Kryptonite Destroyed! "Parents" = They. We use WERE.

Grammar Focus 2

The Negative

To say "NO" in the past, we add NOT.

  • Was + Not → Wasn't
    ("He wasn't an ordinary boy.")
  • Were + Not → Weren't
    ("They weren't sad.")

Grammar Focus 3

Asking Questions

To ask a question, Was and Were move to the front!

Was / Were + Person + Place/Feeling + ?
He was an alien.
Was he an alien?
They were farmers.
Were they farmers?
